D1 / DERIVATION
Causal self-attention for sequences
↳ corresponds to M22 S3: SASRec
Real-life analogy

Imagine reading a mystery novel. At chapter 5, you recall specific earlier clues that now seem relevant — the locked door in chapter 2, the missing key in chapter 3. You don't recall every detail equally; you selectively attend to the clues that matter for the current plot point. SASRec does the same: at position 4 in the browsing sequence, it attends selectively to earlier items, weighting each by relevance to predicting what comes next. But crucially, it can't read ahead — chapter 5 can't attend to chapter 6 (causal masking).

Scaled dot-product attention (causal)
$$\text{Attention}(Q, K, V) = \text{softmax}\left(\frac{QK^\top}{\sqrt{d}} + M\right) V$$
$Q, K, V$Query, Key, Value matrices. For self-attention: $Q = EW^Q, K = EW^K, V = EW^V$ where $E$ is the sequence of item embeddings + position encodings. Each item "queries" all other items, "keys" represent what each item offers, "values" are the content to aggregate
$QK^\top$Attention score matrix $(L \times L)$. Entry $(i,j)$ = how much position $i$ attends to position $j$. High score = "item at position $j$ is relevant for predicting after position $i$"
$\sqrt{d}$Scaling factor. Without it, dot products grow with $d$ → softmax saturates → gradients vanish. Dividing by $\sqrt{d}$ keeps values in a range where softmax has meaningful gradients
$M$Causal mask: $M_{ij} = 0$ if $j \leq i$ (can attend to past/present), $M_{ij} = -\infty$ if $j > i$ (can't attend to future). After softmax, $-\infty$ becomes 0 — future items are invisible
Positional encoding (learned)
$$\mathbf{e}_t = \mathbf{v}_{s_t} + \mathbf{p}_t$$
$\mathbf{v}_{s_t}$Item embedding for the item at position $t$ in the sequence. Same embedding table as MF — shared across all positions
$\mathbf{p}_t$Learned positional embedding for position $t$. Encodes "this item was the 3rd thing browsed" vs "this was the 1st thing browsed." Without this, the model sees a bag of items (position-unaware). SASRec uses LEARNED positions (a separate embedding table for positions 1..L), not sinusoidal
Why add?Self-attention is permutation-invariant by default — it doesn't know order. Adding position embeddings breaks this symmetry. The model can learn that "the most recent item (high position) matters more for next-item prediction than items from 20 clicks ago (low position)"
D2 / WORKED
Attention on a 4-item Kartify session — by hand
↳ corresponds to M22 S3
Session: Kurta → Dupatta → Earrings → [predict next], d=2
Every step
SETUP

Item embeddings + position encodings (d=2, no projection for simplicity — Q=K=V=E):

$$\mathbf{e}_1 = \mathbf{v}_\text{kurta} + \mathbf{p}_1 = [0.5, 0.3] + [0.1, 0.0] = [0.6, 0.3]$$ $$\mathbf{e}_2 = \mathbf{v}_\text{dupatta} + \mathbf{p}_2 = [0.4, 0.4] + [0.0, 0.1] = [0.4, 0.5]$$ $$\mathbf{e}_3 = \mathbf{v}_\text{earrings} + \mathbf{p}_3 = [0.7, 0.1] + [-0.1, 0.1] = [0.6, 0.2]$$
ATTENTION SCORES (QKᵀ / √d)
$$A_\text{raw} = \frac{E \cdot E^\top}{\sqrt{2}} = \frac{1}{1.414}\begin{bmatrix} 0.36+0.09 & 0.24+0.15 & 0.36+0.06 \\ 0.24+0.15 & 0.16+0.25 & 0.24+0.10 \\ 0.36+0.06 & 0.24+0.10 & 0.36+0.04 \end{bmatrix}$$ $$= \frac{1}{1.414}\begin{bmatrix} 0.45 & 0.39 & 0.42 \\ 0.39 & 0.41 & 0.34 \\ 0.42 & 0.34 & 0.40 \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} 0.318 & 0.276 & 0.297 \\ 0.276 & 0.290 & 0.240 \\ 0.297 & 0.240 & 0.283 \end{bmatrix}$$
CAUSAL MASK + SOFTMAX

Apply mask: position $i$ cannot attend to positions $j > i$:

$$A_\text{masked} = \begin{bmatrix} 0.318 & -\infty & -\infty \\ 0.276 & 0.290 & -\infty \\ 0.297 & 0.240 & 0.283 \end{bmatrix}$$

Softmax per row:

$$\text{Row 1: } \text{softmax}([0.318]) = [1.000]$$ $$\text{Row 2: } \text{softmax}([0.276, 0.290]) = [0.497, 0.503]$$ $$\text{Row 3: } \text{softmax}([0.297, 0.240, 0.283]) = [0.353, 0.333, 0.348] \approx [0.347, 0.327, 0.342]$$

Row 3 (earrings position) attends most to kurta (0.347) and itself (0.342), slightly less to dupatta (0.327). All three items are relevant for predicting what comes after earrings.

OUTPUT AT POSITION 3
$$\mathbf{o}_3 = 0.347 \times [0.6, 0.3] + 0.327 \times [0.4, 0.5] + 0.342 \times [0.6, 0.2]$$ $$= [0.208, 0.104] + [0.131, 0.164] + [0.205, 0.068] = [0.544, 0.336]$$

This is the user's session state after seeing kurta → dupatta → earrings. Use this vector to query the item embedding table for the nearest items → predict sandals, matching clutch, etc.

D3 / ANALYSIS
GRU4Rec vs SASRec — complexity comparison
↳ corresponds to M22 S2 vs S3
Computational complexity per sequence
$$\text{GRU4Rec: } O(L \cdot d^2) \quad \text{— sequential (can't parallelize across positions)}$$ $$\text{SASRec: } O(L^2 \cdot d) \quad \text{— parallel (all positions computed simultaneously)}$$
$L$Sequence length. Kartify: $L = 50$ (last 50 items)
$d$Embedding dimension. Kartify: $d = 128$
GRU: $L \cdot d^2$$50 \times 128^2 = 819{,}200$ ops. But must be sequential — each step depends on the previous hidden state. Can't parallelize across positions on GPU
SASRec: $L^2 \cdot d$$50^2 \times 128 = 320{,}000$ ops. Fully parallel — all attention weights computed in one batched matrix multiply. 2.5× fewer ops AND parallelizable → much faster on GPU
CrossoverSASRec wins when $L < d$ (true for most recsys: $L = 50, d = 128$). For very long sequences ($L > d$), GRU's $O(Ld^2)$ becomes cheaper than SASRec's $O(L^2d)$. But most browsing sessions are short enough that SASRec dominates
D4 / INTERVIEW
Interview problems — sequential models
↳ tests M22
Problem: SASRec for cold-start users
Interview
PROBLEM

A new user arrives and clicks exactly 1 item. SASRec's sequence has length 1. What happens? How does this compare to BPR-MF's cold start?

SOLUTION

With $L=1$: the attention matrix is $1 \times 1$ with value 1.0. The output is simply the item embedding + position encoding. SASRec with one item reduces to item-similarity retrieval — "find items similar to the one you just clicked." This is actually useful cold-start behavior — it's the CBF approach from M07 but with learned embeddings instead of TF-IDF.

BPR-MF cold start: the new user has NO embedding (not in the training data). Must fall back to popularity. SASRec cold start: uses the ITEM embedding of their first click. This is strictly better — SASRec inherits a meaningful starting point from the first interaction, while BPR-MF has nothing until the user is added to the next training batch.

This is a key advantage of sequential models: immediate personalization from the first click. No retraining needed — just feed the new click through the trained SASRec model.
