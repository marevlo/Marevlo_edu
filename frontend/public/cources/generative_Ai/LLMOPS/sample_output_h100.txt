This file shows what correct output looks like for two common scenarios.
Numbers are based on published FA-2 and FA-3 benchmarks (Dao 2023, Shah 2024)
on H100 SXM 80GB with CUDA 12.3, bf16. Your numbers will vary by ±5-15%
depending on kernel version, driver, and thermal state.

Reference these when you don't have GPU access — they let you reason about
whether the course's claims are plausible before running anything yourself.

 INTERPRETATION
──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
 Prefill is compute-bound: FA-3's async GEMM + warp specialization
 pays off most at seq_len >= 4096. Below that, kernel launch overhead
 dominates and FA-2 and FA-3 are indistinguishable.

WHAT TO NOTICE
  •  Vanilla OOMs at seq=16384 because the full QK^T matrix is 4*32*16384^2
     = 33.5GB in FP32 (FA keeps this tiled in SRAM).
  •  FA-2 vs FA-3 gap is tiny at seq=1024 (launch overhead dominates) and
     roughly 2x at seq >= 16K. The asymmetry is what matters — FA-3's
     advantage scales with sequence length.
  •  At seq=65K, FA-3 is 144ms / batch of 4. That's 36ms per sequence. In
     your TTFT budget that's a large chunk, which is why long-context
     workloads need chunked prefill (M4) to avoid starving decode.

 INTERPRETATION
──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
 Decode has seqlen_q=1: each step is tiny. Kernel launch overhead
 dominates; FA-2 vs FA-3 gap is small. CUDA graphs matter more here
 than the kernel choice.

WHAT TO NOTICE
  •  Decode times are dominated by HBM bandwidth (reading KV cache). At
     seq=16384, KV for one request is ~170MB; reading it at 3.35 TB/s
     bound is 51 microseconds — close to what FA-3 achieves.
  •  FA-2 and FA-3 are within ~15% of each other in decode. That's the
     gap between "well-tuned HBM-bound kernel" and "slightly better
     tuned HBM-bound kernel". Neither can beat HBM physics.
  •  The batch-32 / 8-heads-KV / d=128 config means we're loading KV for
     32*8=256 head slots per step. That's the right shape for GQA to
     pay off — with MHA (h_kv=64 instead of 8) this would be 8x more KV
     bandwidth.

WHAT THIS TELLS YOU ABOUT YOUR DEPLOYMENT

•  If your prefill is dominated by short prompts (<1K tokens), FA-2 and
   FA-3 will perform nearly identically. Don't upgrade for the 15% — the
   upgrade cost (flash-attn 2.6+ build, H100 required, possible kernel
   incompatibilities with custom attention ops) is rarely worth it.

•  If your prefill runs long contexts (4K+), FA-3 is a meaningful win
   (2-3x) and the upgrade is worth it. This is the RAG / long-document /
   code-assistant case.

•  If you're decode-bound (chat serving, small-batch responses), your
   bottleneck is HBM bandwidth, not the attention kernel. FA-3 helps
   marginally. Bigger wins live in KV cache (M4), speculative decoding
   (M5), or a smaller model (M6).

•  The vanilla numbers exist primarily as a sanity check. Nobody deploys
   vanilla attention. The "10x faster" claim you see in FA papers is
   against vanilla at specific seq lengths — always verify the baseline.
